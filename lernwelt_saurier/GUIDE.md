# Player Guide — Saurier-Forscher (Dino Researcher) 🦕⛏️

This guide explains **all the elements** of the "Saurier-Forscher" learning world,
**how to use them** and **what the tasks and goals are**. Made for **parents and
children playing together** (about 5–7 years old).

> Note: the in-game texts are in **German** (the theme is authored in German), so
> names you see on screen are German; this guide adds English explanations. German
> names appear with an English gloss where helpful.

> Motto: **Entdecken · Erforschen · Vergleichen** (Discover · Research · Compare)
>
> There is no "winning" — the point is to discover, research and learn: watch and name
> dinosaurs, dig up fossils, compare sizes, solve little tasks and hatch eggs, while
> practising counting, patterns, sorting and naming (Swiss curriculum *Lehrplan 21*,
> cycle 1 — mainly science (NMG) and maths / sizes).

---

## 1. Quick start

1. Create an ordinary world (hills, sand and some green work best) — see
   **[WELT-EINRICHTEN.md](WELT-EINRICHTEN.md)**.
2. Make a new world with **VoxeLibre** or **Mineclonia** and enable the mods
   **`lernwelt`** + **`lernwelt_saurier`**.
3. Enter the world → you automatically get the **starter kit**. Fast start: type the
   command **`/saurier_forscherstation`** — a ready-made station with boards, a jeep and
   a dig field appears in front of you.

---

## 2. Basic controls

| Action | How |
|---|---|
| **Use / greet a dino / open a board** | **Right-click** |
| **Photograph / dig up a fossil / break blocks** | **Left-click** (hold) |
| **Build / place an item / plant an egg** | **Right-click** on the ground |
| **Fly (creative)** | Double-jump; then jump = up, sneak = down |

In the child setting, **damage and dying are off** — nothing bad can happen. The time
of day is fixed (no scary night), there are no monsters, and every dino is a friendly
**herbivore**.

---

## 3. The world: 3 learning zones

The base is a **Forscherstation** (research station). Each zone is a "habitat" with a
**learning board** and matching dinos:

| Zone | Dinos there | Learning areas |
|---|---|---|
| 🌿 **Tal** (valley) | Brachiosaurus, Diplodocus, Triceratops, Stegosaurus, Parasaurolophus, Ankylosaurus, Iguanodon, *Rainbow dino* | Science, maths (sizes), German |
| ⛏️ **Fossilien-Grabungsstätte** (fossil dig site) | Pachycephalosaurus, Protoceratops, Microceratus | Science (fossils), maths (counting) |
| 🥚 **Nistplatz** (nesting place) | Maiasaura, Apatosaurus, Hadrosaurus | Maths (counting, sizes), science |

---

## 4. Learning boards & tasks (the heart of the game)

**Use:** right-click a learning board → a window shows the **activity**, the
**curriculum links** and the zone's **tasks**.

Three task types:

- ❓ **Quiz** — a question with answer buttons. Right = ✓ + reward, wrong = "try again".
- 🎨 **Copy the pattern** — a pattern is shown at the top (e.g. small-medium-large or
  egg-mum-egg). Click the buttons **in the right order**. A mistake resets it.
- 🦕 **Research goal** — e.g. "research 2 Triceratops". The progress (x/N) counts up
  automatically as you greet the dinos.

Solved tasks give a sound + praise and count towards your progress
(see `/lernfortschritt`).

**Which tasks are there?**

| Zone | Tasks |
|---|---|
| Valley | Quiz "who has the long neck?" · Quiz "who is bigger?" · **pattern** small-medium-large · Research 2 Triceratops |
| Dig site | Quiz "what do you dig up?" · Quiz **classify** "plants or meat?" · Quiz "how many horns?" · Research 2 Protoceratops |
| Nesting place | Quiz "how do dinos hatch?" · Quiz "how many eggs? (counting)" · Research 1 Maiasaura · **pattern** egg-mum-egg |

## 5. Researching dinos 🦕

**Use:** right-click a dino = **greet & research**. You get hearts, a sound, a counter
entry and (after enough encounters) a new rank.

**All dinos:**

| Dino | Home | Eats | Note | Size |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Brachiosaurus | Valley | Leaves from treetops | very long neck | very big |
| Diplodocus | Valley | Ferns & leaves | whip tail · 👪 family | very big |
| Triceratops | Valley | Low plants | three horns | medium |
| Stegosaurus | Valley | Soft plants | bony plates | medium |
| Parasaurolophus | Valley | Leaves & twigs | trumpet crest | medium |
| Ankylosaurus | Valley | Ground plants | armour & tail club | medium |
| Iguanodon | Valley | All kinds of plants | thumb spike | medium |
| Pachycephalosaurus | Dig site | Leaves & berries | thick bony skull | medium |
| Protoceratops | Dig site | Desert plants | small neck frill · 👪 family | small |
| Microceratus | Dig site | Small plants | tiny & quick | very small |
| Maiasaura | Nesting | Berries & leaves | caring mum · 👪 family | medium |
| Apatosaurus | Nesting | Tree leaves | huge & gentle · 👪 family | very big |
| Hadrosaurus | Nesting | Leaves & cones | duck bill · 👪 family | medium |
| **Regenbogen-Saurier / Rainbow dino** | Valley | Sunlight & pollen | 🌈 very rare, glows | big |

- 👪 **Families:** greet a parent (Diplodocus, Protoceratops, Maiasaura, Apatosaurus,
  Hadrosaurus) and its **babies** appear, which you can research too.
- 🌈 **Rainbow dino:** does **not** appear by itself (very rare). Get it from its
  **spawn egg** (in the starter kit / creative inventory).
- 🌿 **Living valley:** the other dinos **appear automatically** on solid ground near
  you (gently capped). Can be turned off (see Settings).

**Spawn eggs:** every dino has an egg. Egg in hand → **right-click** the ground = place
the dino.

> Note: "researching" works like "rescuing" in the other learning worlds — walk up to a
> dino, click it, and it is counted in your logbook.

---

## 6. Digging up fossils ⛏️ (the researcher's heart)

Real researchers dig fossils out of the ground. Here, **fossils hide in the sand**:

**Use:** **left-click** (hold) to break **Grabungssand** (dig sand). Some sand blocks
contain a **fossil** — it goes into your inventory, a sound plays and your **fossil
counter** rises. That is how you "search" the sand: dig, dig, find!

**Fossils you can find:** dino bone, dino tooth, ammonite, petrified egg.

**Milestones:**

| Fossils found | Title |
|---|---|
| 5 | Fossilien-Sammler (Fossil collector) |
| 15 | Junior-Paläontologe (Junior palaeontologist) |
| 30 | Saurier-Forscher (Dino researcher) |

Get a practice dig field with `/saurier_grabung` or from the test stations. You also
have **Grabungssand** in the starter kit, so you can build your own little dig sites and
let friends search them.

---

## 7. Classifying 🗂️

Researchers sort dinos into groups. At the learning boards (especially the **dig site**)
you practise sorting:

- **Plant-eater or meat-eater?** (here, all dinos are plant-eaters!)
- **Dino groups:** long-necks (Brachiosaurus, Diplodocus, Apatosaurus),
  horned ones (Triceratops, Protoceratops), plated ones (Stegosaurus, Ankylosaurus),
  duck-bills (Parasaurolophus, Hadrosaurus, Maiasaura, Iguanodon),
  thick-skulls (Pachycephalosaurus).

Tip for parents: ask your child to explain **why** a dino belongs to a group (neck,
horns, plates, bill) — that is hands-on classifying.

---

## 8. Comparing sizes 📏 (maths / sizes)

The dinos differ a lot in size — from the tiny **Microceratus** to the huge
**Brachiosaurus**.

- 🧮 **Quiz "who is bigger?"** on the valley board.
- 🎨 **Pattern small-medium-large** on the valley board (click colours in the right
  size order).
- 📏 **Size parade:** `/saurier_groessen` lines the dinos up **from small (left) to big
  (right)** in front of you.
- 📋 The **logbook** lists the dinos with their traits; compare who is bigger.

---

## 9. Hatching eggs 🥚 (nesting place)

**Use:** hold a **dino egg** → **right-click** the ground = plant. After a short time
(about 25 s) a little dino **hatches**. Each hatched dino counts towards your **hatch
counter** (sound + message).

---

## 10. Explorer camera 📷

**Use:** hold the camera, get near a dino and **left-click** = photo. The nearest
**not-yet-discovered** dino is marked **discovered** in the logbook (range about 12
blocks). Photograph **all** dinos — the logbook shows "Discovered: X/14".

---

## 11. Researcher logbook 📋

**Use:** logbook in hand → **right-click** to open. You see a list of all dinos; click
one for the **profile** (name, habitat, eats, note), how **often you researched** it,
and whether it has been **discovered**. The top shows your **rank**, the **total
encounters** and the **discovered count**.

---

## 12. Ranks & badges 🏆

Every encounter counts. At thresholds your rank goes up (sound + pop-up); from
"Junior-Paläontologe" on you also get a **badge item**.

| From … encounters | Rank | Badge |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Frischling (Newcomer) | – |
| 5 | Nachwuchs-Forscher (Trainee researcher) | – |
| 10 | Junior-Paläontologe (Junior palaeontologist) | 🥉 yes |
| 25 | Saurier-Experte (Dino expert) | 🥈 yes |
| 50 | Chef-Paläontologe (Lead palaeontologist) | 🥇 yes |

Top-left a **HUD** always shows: world · rank · encounters.

---

## 13. Forscher-Jeep (research jeep) 🚙

**Use:**
1. **Place** the jeep item (right-click on the ground).
2. **Right-click** the jeep = **get in**.
3. Drive: **W/S** forward/back · **A/D** steer · **jump** = small hop ·
   **right-click** = get out.

**Extras:**
- 💨 **Dust cloud** while driving.
- 👨‍👧 **Second seat** — a **second player** can **ride along** with right-click (ideal
  for parent + child).

## 14. Blocks, items & internal names

Everything is generated without external pictures. Internal names (for the creative
search or `/giveme`) begin with `lernwelt_saurier:`.

**Blocks:** `fels` · `sandstein` · `wuestensand` · `blattwerk` · `vulkangestein` ·
`bernstein` (glows) · `stationsglas` (transparent) · `grabungssand` (fossils to dig) ·
`dino_ei` (hatches) · `tafel_tal/grabung/nistplatz` (learning boards).

**Patterned build blocks:** `muster_fussabdruck` (footprint), `muster_knochen` (bone),
`muster_ei` (nest), `muster_schuppen` (scales), `muster_blatt` (fern), `muster_lava`,
`muster_schach` (checker), `forscherkiste` (a real crate with a lid on top),
`buchstabe_a` … `buchstabe_z` (**letter blocks A–Z**, lay out **dino names**) and
`zahl_0` … `zahl_9` (**number blocks 0–9**, for counting). In creative mode all blocks
are in the build menu.

> **Own patterns or pictures on blocks?** Yes! A block can use `texture = "..."`
> instead of `color = "#..."`:
> - **Pattern without a file** — layer colour rectangles, e.g.
>   `texture = "[fill:16x16:#e4c690^[fill:8x8:0,0:#5d4037^[fill:8x8:8,8:#5d4037"`
>   (checker). Overlay format: `^[fill:WIDTHxHEIGHT:X,Y:#colour`.
> - **A real picture** — put a PNG (16×16 or 32×32) in the `textures/` folder and set
>   `texture = "lernwelt_saurier_mypic.png"`.
> - **Per face** — `top`, `side` and `bottom` give a block different faces (like the
>   `forscherkiste`). See `textures/README.txt`.

**Items:** `logbuch` · `kamera` · `jeep` · the fossils (`knochen`, `dino_zahn`,
`ammonit`, `ei_fossil`) · the spawn eggs (e.g. `triceratops`, `maiasaura`,
`regenbogensaurier`) · badges (`badge_10/25/50`).

## 16. Settings

In the menu under **Settings → All settings → Mods** (or in `minetest.conf`):

| Setting | Default | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| `lernwelt_show_hud` | on | Progress display top-left |
| `lernwelt_spawn_creatures` | on | Dinos appear automatically on solid ground |
| `lernwelt_big_text` | off | **Larger text** on the learning boards |
| `lernwelt_easy_mode` | off | **Easy mode**: quizzes show only 2 answers |
| `lernwelt_saurier_starter_kit` | on | Starter kit on first join |

## 17. For parents & teachers 👪

- **Lehrplan 21 (cycle 1):** every zone and task is tagged with competence areas —
  **science (NMG)** (animals & habitats, how researchers explore the past), **maths**
  (counting, patterns, **comparing & ordering sizes**), German (naming), movement.
- `/lernplan` shows all zones and their links at a glance.
- `/lernfortschritt` shows per child: rank, encounters, tasks solved, dinos met and the
  learning areas covered.
- **Accessibility:** `lernwelt_big_text` (large text) and `lernwelt_easy_mode` (fewer
  quiz options) make it easier to start.

## 18. Little missions & ideas

- 🟢 **First steps:** research 5 dinos → rank "Nachwuchs-Forscher".
- ⛏️ **Excavator:** dig up 15 fossils → "Junior-Paläontologe".
- 📷 **Researcher:** photograph all dinos (Discovered 14/14).
- 🧩 **Puzzler:** solve all tasks on all three boards.
- 📏 **Size master:** use `/saurier_groessen` and name every dino from small to big.
- 🔤 **Name play:** build your favourite dino's name with letter blocks.
- 🥚 **Hatcher:** hatch three eggs at the nesting place.
- 🌈 **Legend:** find the rare rainbow dino.
- 👨‍👧 **Team expedition:** drive the jeep together.

## 19. Troubleshooting

- **Dinos don't appear automatically:** `lernwelt_spawn_creatures` must be on and a mob
  API is required (VoxeLibre/Mineclonia or `mobs_redo`). You can always place dinos with
  **spawn eggs**.
- **No sound:** sound files are optional. Put your own `.ogg` files in the `sounds/`
  folder (see `sounds/README.txt`) — without them everything works, just silently.
- **I can't find fossils:** you must dig **Grabungssand** (not ordinary desert sand).
  Lay out a dig field with `/saurier_grabung` or build your own from your dig sand.
- **"Unknown node":** if something is missing, check that both mods (`lernwelt` +
  `lernwelt_saurier`) are enabled.

Have fun discovering, researching and comparing! 🦕⛏️
